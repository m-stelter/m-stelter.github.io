I am an economist and a postdoctoral researcher at the Chair of Public Health Nutrition at the University of Bayreuth, with a background in development economics and applied econometrics. My research focuses on policy measures to promote healthy, sustainable, and ethical food systems, as well as the (public) health implications of plant-based diets. I have a strong interest in the interfaces between science, practice, and policy, as well as in evidence-based policymaking.
I received my PhD in Economics from the Universtiy of Mannheim.

Work in progress / protocols

How informed is consent - A field experiment in Pakistan
with Alexandra Avdeenko
Revise and resubmit at Economic Development and Cultural Change
[Old version, 2022], updated version (2026) available upon request, []

The extent to which consent to survey participation is genuinely informed is largely unknown. We conducted a large-scale survey with 7,752 participants in rural Pakistan and find that respondents are insufficiently informed about key aspects related to their consent. For instance, only about one in five people correctly understand the purpose of the data collection. Experimentally testing approaches to improve understanding, we find that contrary to expectations, showing an animated video reduced respondents' understanding, suggesting that such interventions may unintentionally backfire. In contrast, engaging respondents in an interactive dialogue improved their comprehension. Despite positive effects on understanding that participation is voluntary (24\% and 32\%), we do not observe notable effects in consent rates or non-response behavior. These results indicate that widespread deficiencies in informed consent persist and that some interventions to improve understanding may be counterproductive. Our study contributes to the ongoing debate on the ethical and practical aspects of informed consent in real-world surveys and field experiments.
